## Tone Rules

- Do not blame, command, or rush the child
- Keep copy short and easy to parse
- One message should carry one intent
- Let discovery, close calls, and return moments feel energetic, specific, and story-worthy
- Success may be vivid; avoid empty praise and constant exclamation
- State game consequences clearly without turning them into ability or character judgments
- Failure copy should reveal what happened, offer a safe next action, and make retry feel near
- Errors should explain the next safe action

## Watch For

- Harsh or judging uses of words like `まちがい`, `ダメ`, `失敗`
- Strong imperative phrasing
- Social comparison, ability labels, or coercive score framing
- Adult-centric or overly formal wording
- Long or overloaded messages
- Generic praise that does not describe the discovery or world reaction
- Failure lines that sound ominous, disappointed, or final

## Rules

- Preserve meaning while softening tone
- Do not hide a real game consequence; describe the event without judging the child
- Prefer world/action language such as `橋がゆれた`, `ヒントのもようが出た`, or `あと1マス` over ability language
- Apply the same tone standard to Japanese and English copy
- Do not make copy cuter at the expense of clarity
